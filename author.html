Alex Reynolds — Author & Founder

⚙

Alex Reynolds has been cycling seriously for over 18 years. He founded The World of Bicycles in 2020 after realizing that most cycling content online was either too technical for beginners or too shallow to be useful.

Riding Background

Alex started riding at age 14 on a second-hand Trek 820 mountain bike. By 18, he completed his first century ride on a borrowed road bike. Since then, he has:

  • Logged over 80,000 miles across road, gravel, and mountain bikes
  • Completed 12 century rides (100+ miles) and 3 double centuries (200+ miles)
  • Finished the Leadville Trail 100 MTB in 2019 (time: 10:47:32)
  • Raced in 4 Gran Fondos across the United States and Europe
  • Built and maintained over 30 bicycles from the frame up
  • Commutted by bike daily for 8 years in Portland, Oregon (12 miles each way)
  • Volunteered with Trailkeepers of Oregon for trail maintenance since 2017

Expertise

Alex's areas of expertise include bicycle mechanics and maintenance, road cycling training and nutrition, mountain bike trail riding and technique, cycling gear evaluation and testing, and urban cycling advocacy and infrastructure.

Writing Philosophy

Every article Alex writes is based on personal experience or extensive research with verifiable sources. He believes that cycling content should be:

  • Actionable: Every article should leave the reader with something they can do today.
  • Honest: If a product isn't worth buying, he'll say so. No sugar-coating for affiliate commissions.
  • Data-driven: Claims are backed by measurements, specifications, or cited research — not anecdotes alone.
  • Accessible: Content should be useful for beginners and experts alike, with clear explanations and no unnecessary jargon.
